### Notable Findings

**1. Boolean overlap threshold at ~42%:**
Pair 2v4 (42.9% overlap, 3 shared strong peaks) is the only pair that fails Boolean — AND=89%, XOR=89%. All other pairs (≤40% overlap) score 100%. This maps to a design rule: keep spectral overlap below ~40% for threshold-based Boolean.

**2. NN is more overlap-tolerant than Boolean:**
Pair 2v4 fails Boolean at 93% but passes NN at 100% (11/11). Template matching aggregates across all 10 enrolled peaks, diluting the effect of shared modes. Boolean makes per-frequency binary decisions and is thus more fragile to spectral overlap.

**3. Noise robustness — monotonically improving margins:**
| Drive | Margin |
|-------|--------|
| 100% (2.0 Vpp) | +2.5 |
| 80% (1.6 Vpp) | +2.8 |
| 60% (1.2 Vpp) | +3.0 |
| 40% (0.8 Vpp) | +3.1 |
| 20% (0.4 Vpp) | +3.4 |
| 10% (0.2 Vpp) | +4.2 |

Counter-intuitive: lower drive → better discrimination. Template scoring normalizes by total power, so reduced excitation may actually reduce mode coupling. Good news for MEMS power budgets.

**4. 3-input Boolean and chained gates:**
First demonstration of:
- 3-input gates (AND3, OR3, MAJ, XOR3) — all 100%
- Composable logic: (A AND B) XOR C = 100%
- Frequency-domain computation composes without signal regeneration

**5. Universal crossover at α=0.3→0.6:**
All 6 NN pairs cross over in the same α range. This is determined by mode spacing, not overlap. Implies a single α threshold could work for any rod pair — no per-pair calibration needed.
