PZT Transducer Placement (all plates):
• TX (transmit) PZT disc: bottom-left corner at (5, 95) mm — bond to plate underside with cyanoacrylate
• RX (receive) PZT disc: top-right corner at (95, 5) mm — bond to plate underside (same face as TX)
• Both PZTs on underside — leaves entire top surface clear for perturbation sites
• Extreme corner placement — corners are nodal for most bending modes → preserves Q ≈ 22,500 (see analysis)
• PZT disc: 10 mm dia × 1 mm thick, flush with two plate edges at each corner
• Diagonal-opposite corners maximize acoustic path. Orange ◆ = TX, Blue ◆ = RX on each diagram

Foam Support Placement (all plates):
• 3-point support — statically determinate, no over-constraint
• Positions at nodal intersections of (2,0)×(0,2) modes: (22, 22), (78, 22), and (50, 78) mm
• Soft foam pads (~5 mm × 5 mm × 3 mm thick) — green □ on each diagram
• These points have near-zero displacement for all fundamental bending modes → minimal Q damping
• Triangle base faces upward, apex downward — stable against tipping, clears both PZTs

Pattern Design Rationale (from E38 3-condition results):
• Perturbation location matters more than mass — sites must sit near mode antinodes
• Each pattern targets different mode families of a free square plate (Chladni patterns)
• Pattern A: (1,1) + (2,2) antinodes — center + quarter-points
• Pattern B: (2,1) + (1,2) antinodes — edge midpoints
• Pattern C: (3,3) grid — higher-order mode antinodes at third-points
• Pattern D/E (duplicate): Diagonal — asymmetric modes, breaks (m,n)↔(n,m) degeneracy
