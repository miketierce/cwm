CWM Breadboard Wiring Guide

Nano Ard2 (DDS) at D2–H16  |  AD9833 boards pins in col C  |  Ard1 (relay) is off-board

How a Breadboard Works

Each row has TWO groups of 5 holes, separated by the center gap:

  LEFT SIDE              GAP           RIGHT SIDE
  A  B  C  D  E          |          F  G  H  I  J
  ●──●──●──●──●                     ●──●──●──●──●     <-- Row N

The 5 holes on each side are connected by a metal strip INSIDE.
  A5, B5, C5, D5, E5 = one electrical node.
  F5, G5, H5, I5, J5 = another node.

To connect LEFT to RIGHT you must bridge the gap with a wire.

The + and - rails along the top/bottom run the full board length.

IMPORTANT: Only ONE wire fits in each hole!
If two wires need the same row, use two DIFFERENT holes
in that row -- the board connects them for you.
Your Arduino Nano — What Pin Is Where

Nano is plugged in at rows 2–16, pins in columns D (left) and H (right).

D (left) H (right) +------------------+ Row 2 -- | D13/SCK D12 | Row 3 -- | 3V3 D11/MOSI| Row 4 -- | AREF D10/FS1 | Row 5 -- | A0 D9/FS2 | Row 6 -- | A1 D8 | Row 7 -- | A2 D7 | Row 8 -- | A3 D6 | Row 9 -- | A4 D5 | Row 10 -- | A5 D4 | Row 11 -- | A6 D3 | Row 12 -- | A7 D2 | Row 13 -- | 5V GND | Row 14 -- | RST RST | Row 15 -- | GND RX0 | Row 16 -- | VIN TX1 | +------------------+ USB at top (row 2 end)

Left pins (col D) are accessible from columns A, B, C (same row, left side).
Right pins (col H) are accessible from columns I, J (same row, right side).
Columns E, F, G are under the Nano body — you can't plug into them.

1 Power — 2 wires
# From To What Wire Color
① C13 + rail Nano 5V → power rail Red
② C15 - rail Nano GND → ground rail Black

C13 is connected to D13 (the Nano's 5V pin) inside the breadboard.
C15 is connected to D15 (the Nano's GND pin) inside the breadboard.

2 SPI Bus — 2 wires

We create a "CLK bus" at row 19 and a "DAT bus" at row 20. Both AD9833 boards tap into these later.

# From To What Wire Color
③ A2 A19 SCK → clock bus (straight down col A) Blue
④ I3 A20 MOSI → data bus (right side, around, to left) Green

③ — A2 connects to D2 (SCK) inside the board. Wire runs straight down column A to row 19.
④ — I3 connects to H3 (MOSI). Route wire along the right edge, down past the Nano, then across to A20 on the left.

After step 2, the bus rows look like this: Row 19 (CLK): A19 -- B19 -- C19 -- D19 -- E19 wire ③ is in A19 (③) Row 20 (DAT): A20 -- B20 -- C20 -- D20 -- E20 wire ④ is in A20 (④) 4 holes free in each row for the AD9833 boards.
3 FSYNC Wires — 2 wires
# From To What Wire Color
⑤ I4 A26 D10 → Board #1 FSYNC Orange
⑥ I5 A36 D9 → Board #2 FSYNC Purple

⑤ — I4 connects to H4 (D10). Route along the right edge, down, then across to A26.
(A26 connects to C26 where Board #1's FSYNC pin will be.)

⑥ — I5 connects to H5 (D9). Same routing to A36.
(A36 connects to C36 where Board #2's FSYNC pin will be.)

These are the longest wires. Route them along the outside edge of the board to keep things tidy.

4 AD9833 Board #1 — plug in + 6 wires

First: Plug Board #1's 7-pin header into column C, rows 22–28. The board body covers columns D, E, and part of F/G. That's fine — all wires route through columns A and B.

Pin Hole Signal
1 (top) C22 VCC
2 C23 DGND
3 C24 SDATA
4 C25 SCLK
5 C26 FSYNC — already wired via ⑤ (at A26)
6 C27 AGND
7 (bottom) C28 OUT

# From To What Wire Color
⑦ A22 + rail VCC → power Red
⑧ A23 - rail DGND → ground Black
⑨ B24 B20 SDATA → DAT bus Green
⑩ B25 B19 SCLK → CLK bus Blue
⑪ A27 - rail AGND → ground Black
⑫ A28 A42 OUT → summing row 42 Yellow

No FSYNC wire here — already done in step 3 (wire ⑤ at A26 connects to C26).

After step 4, the bus rows: Row 19 (CLK): A19 -- B19 -- C19 -- D19 -- E19 3 holes still free (③) (⑩) Row 20 (DAT): A20 -- B20 -- C20 -- D20 -- E20 3 holes still free (④) (⑨)
5 AD9833 Board #2 — plug in + 6 wires

First: Plug Board #2's 7-pin header into column C, rows 32–38.

Pin Hole Signal
1 (top) C32 VCC
2 C33 DGND
3 C34 SDATA
4 C35 SCLK
5 C36 FSYNC — already wired via ⑥ (at A36)
6 C37 AGND
7 (bottom) C38 OUT

# From To What Wire Color
⑬ A32 + rail VCC → power Red
⑭ A33 - rail DGND → ground Black
⑮ B34 C20 SDATA → DAT bus Green
⑯ B35 C19 SCLK → CLK bus Blue
⑰ A37 - rail AGND → ground Black
⑱ A38 A43 OUT → summing row 43 Yellow

Note the different holes! Board #2's data goes to C20 (not B20) and clock goes to C19 (not B19) because Board #1 already used B20 and B19. Same row = same signal, just a different hole.

After step 5, the bus rows: Row 19 (CLK): A19 -- B19 -- C19 -- D19 -- E19 2 holes still free (③) (⑩) (⑯) Row 20 (DAT): A20 -- B20 -- C20 -- D20 -- E20 2 holes still free (④) (⑨) (⑮) All 3 wires in each row carry the same signal -- the board does it.
6 Summing Network — 2 bridges + 3 resistors + PicoScope + PZT

This is where the 3 signals get combined. Uses the right half of rows 42–45.

How the Summing Works

The right half of each row (F-G-H-I-J) is one connected strip:

Row 42:  F42 -- G42 -- H42 -- I42 -- J42    OUT1 arrives here
Row 43:  F43 -- G43 -- H43 -- I43 -- J43    OUT2 arrives here
Row 44:  F44 -- G44 -- H44 -- I44 -- J44    AWG arrives here
Row 45:  F45 -- G45 -- H45 -- I45 -- J45    SUM node (to PZT)

Each resistor connects one signal row DOWN to the sum row.
Each leg goes in its OWN hole. Row 45 ties them together.

               Row 42     Row 43     Row 44
              (OUT1)     (OUT2)     (AWG)
                |          |          |
                R1         R2         R3
               10k        10k        10k
                |          |          |
                +----------+----------+--- Row 45 = SUM
                                              |
                                         PZT drive out

6a. Bridge the gap (OUT signals are on the left, resistors on the right)

# From To What
⑲ E42 F42 Bridge OUT1 across gap to right side
⑳ E43 F43 Bridge OUT2 across gap to right side

Short jumper wires crossing the center gap.
E42 is on the left side (connected to A42 where OUT1 arrived via wire ⑫).
F42 is the right side (where the resistor will connect).

6b. Three 10kΩ resistors

# Leg 1 (signal) Leg 2 (sum) What
㉑ G42 F45 R1 — OUT1 to sum (spans 3 rows)
㉒ G43 G45 R2 — OUT2 to sum (spans 2 rows)
㉓ G44 H45 R3 — AWG to sum (spans 1 row)

Bend the resistor leads as needed.
The three bottom legs land in F45, G45, H45 — three different holes, but all connected because they're in the same row (45, right side).

6c. PicoScope AWG

# From To What
㉔ PicoScope AWG hot wire F44 AWG signal → row 44 right side
㉕ PicoScope AWG gnd wire - rail AWG ground → ground rail

6d. Output to PZT

# From To What
㉖ I45 PZT drive wire (hot) Combined signal → piezo
㉗ PZT gnd wire - rail Piezo ground → ground rail
Final state of summing rows (right half only): Row 42: F42 -- G42 -- H42 -- I42 -- J42 (⑲) (R1 top) Row 43: F43 -- G43 -- H43 -- I43 -- J43 (⑳) (R2 top) Row 44: F44 -- G44 -- H44 -- I44 -- J44 (㉔) (R3 top) Row 45: F45 -- G45 -- H45 -- I45 -- J45 SUM NODE (R1 bot) (R2 bot) (R3 bot) (㉖) Every used hole has exactly ONE thing in it. Row 45 connects them all automatically.
Signal Flow Summary
AD9833 #1 --OUT(C28)--> A28 --> A42 --> E42 --bridge--> F42 then R1(10k): G42 --> F45 --+ | AD9833 #2 --OUT(C38)--> A38 --> A43 --> E43 --bridge--> F43 then R2(10k): G43 --> G45 --+-- ROW 45 = SUM | PicoScope AWG hot --> F44 | --> I45 then R3(10k): G44 --> H45 --+ --> PZT drive
