CWM Kernel Experiment — Wiring Diagram

2× AD9833 DDS (Ard2) + PicoScope 2204A AWG → Resistive Sum → PZT Drive  |  PicoScope Ch A = Sense

+5 V (red)
GND (black)
SPI CLK — D13 (blue)
SPI DAT — D11 (green)
FSYNC #1 — D10 (orange)
FSYNC #2 — D9 (purple)
Analog OUT (gold)
PicoScope AWG (gray)
Summed signal (dark red)
  • Power safety: Keep + rail and − rail on the SAME SIDE of the breadboard. Do NOT bridge across sides.
  • CLK & DAT daisy-chain (dashed lines) — both boards share the same bus row on the breadboard.
  • FSYNC wires are individual — each board gets its own dedicated wire to the Nano. These are the chip-select lines.
  • Summing resistors: 3× 10kΩ, equal value. Gives ~200mVpp combined. If too quiet, drop to 3× 1kΩ.
  • PicoScope AWG amplitude: Set to 600,000 µVpp (600 mVpp) to match AD9833 output level.
  • Board #3 slot (D8): Leave unused. Firmware will respond "DDS3:0" to queries — harmless.
  • Sense path: Existing relay mux wiring from April 8 session is unchanged. Sense PZT → relay → Ch A.
  • Ground star: Pico AWG GND, Nano GND, and both AD9833 GNDs must share the same − rail.
