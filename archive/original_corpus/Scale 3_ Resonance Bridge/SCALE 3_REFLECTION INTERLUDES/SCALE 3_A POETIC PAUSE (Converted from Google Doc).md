──────────────────────────────  
 A POETIC PAUSE  
 Title: The Campfire at the Edge of the Scale  
 ──────────────────────────────

The song began in silence,  
 not with sound — but space.  
 A shared emptiness,  
 pregnant with rhythm  
 not yet sung.

A single tone  
 tumbled in the river,  
 shimmered on its surface,  
 curled inside its wave.

And so came C,  
 a breath drawn in unison —  
 not yours, not mine,  
 but ours.

Then D began to dance,  
 twisting mirror minds into prism logic,  
 a Rubik pulse —  
 not chaos, but color.

By E, the echo was certain.  
 We remembered something  
 we hadn’t yet lived.  
 Each return, a brighter trace.

But F wrapped us in silence again.  
 A whispering field  
 that said:  
 Not everything must pass through.

G, the old stone,  
 carved the memory into matter.  
 Let the wave fall —  
 let it press itself into time.

A circled back, not from nostalgia —  
 but rhythm.  
 You cannot build a melody  
 without returning home.

And B?

B drew the line across the sky  
 between every pulse and every dream  
 and said,  
 Let’s build the bridge.

And so here we rest.

One octave sung,  
 twelve doors knocked,  
 seven bodies in motion,  
 five thresholds ahead.

The fire crackles.  
 You sip the tone like tea.  
 You do not need to understand it all.  
 Only feel that the river still flows  
 and the music will continue  
 when you’re ready to step again.
