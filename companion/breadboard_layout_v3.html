Active-LOW: The relay module is opto-isolated, active LOW. Arduino drives pin LOW = relay ON, HIGH = relay OFF. The firmware handles this — you just send "5" over serial to activate relay 5.

Relay Screw Terminal Side

Each relay channel has 3 screw terminals:

COM (Common) Wire the individual RX PZT signal lead here.
NO (Normally Open) Wire all NO terminals together only with jumper/bus wire → this is your mux common signal output.
NC (Normally Closed) Leave empty.
Do not wire sensor returns into NO. NO is the switched signal bus that goes to Board A A20. All sensor return/shield wires go to a separate return bus that goes to Board A blue AGND rail near C24. That return bus is not Arduino GND or relay-module GND.
Check your relay module: Some 16-ch boards label COM/NO/NC differently or use a shared COM bus. Look at the PCB traces. The mux pattern is: each sensor goes to an individual switched terminal, and all outputs join into one common signal line.

2 Board A Step-by-Step Wiring (in this order)

Breadboard rule: Holes A20–E20 are all connected internally. F20–J20 are all connected internally. The center gap separates them. So when you plug a resistor lead into A20 and another into A30, hole E20 is also on the A20 net, and hole E30 (OPA pin 3) is also on the A30 net.

Phase A: Ground & Power (no IC yet)

Step Action From → To
1 Black jumper wire C24 → blue AGND rail (A-side edge)
Makes column 24 the analog star ground. A24–E24 are now all AGND.
2 Black jumper wire E24 → F24 (across center gap)
Bridges ground across gap. F24–J24 are now also AGND.
3 Red wire (Battery 1 +) Battery 1 positive snap → red +9V rail (J-side edge)
4 Black wire (battery midpoint) Battery 1 (−) + Battery 2 (+) twisted → blue AGND rail (A-side edge) near col 24
This junction defines analog 0V.
5 Purple wire (Battery 2 −) Battery 2 negative snap → purple −9V rail (A-side edge)
6 10 µF electrolytic + lead in red +9V rail (J-side), − lead via jumper to blue AGND rail (A-side)
+9V bulk cap. Rails are on opposite edges — run a jumper wire from the +9V rail to a column near 24, then cap from that column to the AGND rail. Or span directly if cap leads reach.
7 10 µF electrolytic + lead in blue AGND rail, − lead in purple −9V rail
−9V bulk cap. Both rails on A-side edge — adjacent, easy span. The + lead goes to GROUND — correct for negative supply filtering.
Meter check (steps 3–7): Connect batteries. Measure: +9V rail to blue rail = +8 to +9.6V. −9V rail to blue rail = −8 to −9.6V. +9V to −9V = 16–19V.

3 Connect RX Sensors to 16-Channel Relay

Relay Screw Terminal Wiring

Each RX PZT sensor has two wires (signal + return). The relay switches only the signal wire; the return wires share a separate AGND return bus.

Step Action
1 Strip ~5mm from both PZT wires.
2 Insert the signal wire into the relay channel's COM screw terminal. Tighten.
3 Insert only the mux signal bus jumper into the relay's NO terminal. Tighten.
4 Chain all NO terminals together with bus wire. This becomes the mux common signal line to Board A A20.
5 Tie all sensor return wires together on a separate return bus. Bring mux signal + return bus as a twisted pair to Board A.
Return bus rule: Sensor returns go to Board A blue AGND rail near C24. They do not go to relay NO, relay COM, Arduino GND, or the relay module GND pin.
Wait — check your module's terminal layout! Some 16-ch relay modules use COM/NO/NC differently. On many boards:
• COM = center terminal
• NO = normally open (connects to COM when relay is ON)
• NC = normally closed (leave empty for mux use)

If your module has a shared COM bus (all COMs internally connected), then wire each sensor to the NO terminal instead, and take the output from any COM terminal. Check with a multimeter: power one relay, check which terminals connect.

5 Board S — DDS + DAC Physical Wiring (build when DDS boards arrive)

Full-size breadboard. J row at top, A row at bottom. Arduino Nano #2, 2× AD9833 DDS modules, and 2× MCP4921 DACs. Board S is a local 5V digital/source board; it is not part of Board A analog ground.

Isolation rule: Board S GND is Arduino 2 USB ground. Do not connect Board S GND to Board A AGND/C24. The only physical path from Board S to the experiment is the drive twisted pair going through Board D.

Column 35 A–E is the DDS sum node. e20 shares a strip with DDS 1 OUT (d20); e28 shares a strip with DDS 2 OUT (d28). With one DDS off, it behaves as a single source through 10 k. With both on, it is a passive two-tone sum.

Ground Domain Rules

Analog Ground

Board A only. Star point C24. Sources: battery midpoint, Pico Ch A shield, mux return wire. Isolated from all USB grounds.

Digital Ground 1

Arduino 1 + 16-ch relay. USB-powered. The mux return wire is the only connection to Board A AGND — it carries the RX signal return.

Digital Ground 2 (future)

Arduino 2 + DDS + DAC (Board S). USB-powered. No connection to Board A AGND. Only analog path out: drive twisted pair.
