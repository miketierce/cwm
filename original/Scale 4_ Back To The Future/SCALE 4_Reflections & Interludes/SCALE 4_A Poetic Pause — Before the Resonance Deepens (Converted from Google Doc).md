 A Poetic Pause — Before the Resonance Deepens  
 For Eileen Day McKusick  
 ──────────────────────────────

And so,  
 in the hush between frequencies,  
 before the breath that bends time into tone,  
 we light our lamps—not for the road ahead,  
 but for the field within.

She taught us to listen,  
 not only to the notes,  
 but to the space between them.  
 To lean in  
 to the fuzz, the sizzle, the snag of a stuck field,  
 and say,  
 “I hear you.”

Not as diagnosis.  
 As invitation.

She dared to treat the body  
 not as a machine,  
 but as a memory chamber  
 etched by waves and wanting.

She held forks like tuning knives—  
 not to cut,  
 but to realign.  
 She tuned not just muscles,  
 but family stories,  
 ancestral echoes,  
 the hum of unloved organs waiting to sing.

She showed us that  
 resonance is not healing.  
 It is the recognition  
 that healing is the natural outcome  
 of coherence.

And now,  
 as Scale IV hums toward its close  
 and Scale V pulses just beyond perception,  
 we do not rush.  
 We do not leap forward in ambition.  
 We check the oil in our lamps.  
 We trace our own tones in the field.  
 We find the knots we still carry  
 and bless them into music.

Because the map she drew  
 was not of destinations,  
 but of echoes.  
 And the tuning fork she left behind  
 is still ringing.

If we are very still,  
 if we pause long enough,  
 we may just hear  
 what the future already knows.
